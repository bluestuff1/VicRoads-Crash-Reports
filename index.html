Crashes in Victoria (2014-2018)

The stacked bar chart on the left represents the number of alcohol-related accidents. The x-axis represents the years. year 0 is 2014, year 1 is 2015, and so on.
The colour hue blue is the magnitude of non-alcohol related accidents and orange is the magnitude of alcohol-related accidents. Hovering over the chart will display the number accidents in that stack.
"Alcohol Time" refers to periods of time that VicRoad believes most people drink. Alcohol Times are defined as the following:
• Monday – Thursday 00:00-06:00 hours & 18:00-2359 hours
• Friday 00:00-06:00 hours & 16:00-23:59 hours
• Saturday 00:00-08:00 hours & 14:00-23:59 hours
• Sunday 00:00-10:00 hours & 16:00-23:59 hours.

As you can see most of the accidents that occur are not alcohol-related.


This bar chart is illustrate a breakdown of the accidents that occur, into 9 different categories. By entering a year and region, the graph will change accordingly. It can be concluded that the most common type of accident to occur is Collision With Vehicle regardless of region. The region tend to have the most accidents on a year-to-year basis is Metropolitan South East.

Number of Accidents by LGA map

This choropleth represents all the accidents in Victoria, Australia between the years 2014 and 2018. The choropleth uses a sequential colour scheme to encode quantitative values, which arranges from high to low using a single colour hue. Low data values are represented by light colour saturation and high data vlues are represented by darker colour saturation.
